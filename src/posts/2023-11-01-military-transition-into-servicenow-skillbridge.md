It feels like crunchtime right now. I currently have a lot of thoughts running through my mind every second of the day. Preparing to clear out the military, move my family temporarily to the west coast, all while being two weeks away from the ServiceNow training program.

I’ve decided to document my journey on Medium because its a platform I use often to read raw stories from writers and creators. It's also my way of embedding my “long form” of content, other than YouTube or a Podcast, which I have the urge to start a podcast, but maybe one day.

Overall, I’d love to share a timeline on how I got started in tech.

#### The Early Late Days

Late 2021, living in South Korea, military contract is shortly coming to an end, and I have no clue what I’m going to do with my life. There were various jobs I considered such as electrician, plumber, HVAC, welding, the blue collar job. Working hard at first to one day own a company in the trades wasn’t a dream, but a means to an end.

A friend, [jedcal](https://www.instagram.com/jedcal/), posted a popular video called “[Day in the Life of a Software Engineer (New York Edition)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MFptcZb4YwM)”, and it went viral! I was hooked on the “lifestyle” he portrayed. Although, most folks show the good parts, it was a life I imagined, on top of having a family.

This led me to go deep into learning about how to become a software engineer. I was sucked into the YouTube rabbit hole of finding videos from notable creators such as [Chris Sean](https://www.youtube.com/@RealChrisSean), [Dorian Develops](https://www.youtube.com/@DorianDevelops), [Michael White Dev](https://www.youtube.com/@MichaelWhiteDev), and [Jeremiah Peoples](https://www.youtube.com/@JeremiahPeoples).

Through their videos, I learned about a free way to do this, without a degree, [The Odin Project](https://www.theodinproject.com) (TOP).

The Odin Project is a free, self pace course, that takes you from not knowing any code, to becoming a full stack web developer. You have the option of either learning Ruby on Rails, or going with JavaScript and learning the MERN stack (MongoDB, Express, React, Node).

I learned quickly that it was difficult to learn on my own, and asking for help wasnt easy for me. I gave up on TOP because I could not grasp the concept of if/else statements and functions in JavaScript.

#### The Come Up — Reddit

A guy on the internet named [Leon Noel](https://leonnoel.com/100devs/) posted on the [r/learnprogramming](https://www.reddit.com/r/learnprogramming/?rdt=56292) subreddit about [running a second cohort for their 30-week full stack software engineering bootcamp](https://www.reddit.com/r/learnprogramming/comments/rlmulr/i_ran_a_100_free_full_stack_web_development/) called [100Devs](https://www.reddit.com/r/learnprogramming/comments/rlmulr/i_ran_a_100_free_full_stack_web_development/).

![](https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/800/1*pzcl3aTSFe0t7LURGqTmmQ.jpeg)

Leon Noel’s post

It was at this moment I shared this post that I was going to participate.

#### Fast Forward

Summer of 2022, I’m trying to find what training program I would like to complete before my military service is over. I’ve attended various networking events with [50Strong](https://www.50-strong.us), [ACP](https://www.acp-usa.org), [Hiring Our Heroes](https://www.hiringourheroes.org/?utm_source=HOH&utm_medium=Cvent&utm_campaign=registration&utm_id=Cvent), and [Hire Heroes](https://www.hireheroesusa.org). But it was either a post or video I saw on YouTube about ServiceNow that I was hooked to pursue. The ability to create a full stack application and customize within minutes was game changing. So I researched, how can I learn about the platform?

ServiceNow has many different [NextGen](https://www.servicenow.com/company/global-impact/social/digital-readiness/nextgen.html) training programs, but I was eligible for the 16-week cohort for Veterans, Spouses, and Transitioning Service Members. Before committing, I did my due diligence to research.

Research consisted of YouTube, LinkedIn cold messages, virtual coffee chats, finding people on X (Twitter for the OGs), Slack / Discord channels, and in-person meetups in my area.

Safe to say, I receive both, positives and negatives on the matter, and the positives outweighed the negative. I attended a webinar with [Herb Thompson](https://www.linkedin.com/in/herb-thompson-sf2biz/), applied with all the documents, and got accepted to begin November 14, 2023!

#### Overall

I start the cohort in two weeks, grown a circle of like-minded folks that are ready to tackle the cohort, reached out to veterans and beginners of the platform, and learned the free material on the Now Learning platform.

My goals are hefty for this cohort, but I’d love to share them for accountability:

1. Earn the ServiceNow Certified System Administrator(CSA), Certified Application Developer(CAD), and 1x Certified Implementation Specialist(CIS) certification.
2. Earn Professional Scrum Master I (PSM 1)
3. Earn ITIL v4

I’m absolutely happy, excited, and nervous to be going through this training program because its the bittersweet feeling of my chapter in the military coming to an end, and a new door opening up, not only for myself, but my family.

I applaud you for making it this far! I will be posting my journey of breaking into tech, specifically ServiceNow, weekly with you folks! Thank you for taking the time to read and look forward to your thoughts and comments!
