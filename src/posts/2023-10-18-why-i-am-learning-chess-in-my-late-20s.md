title: Why I Am Learning Chess in My Late 20s
description: And no it's not because of The Queen's Gambit
date: 2023-10-17
---

I never cared about chess. I saw it as a hobby for grandpas in parks or gifted kids in school. Now I spend my nights studying pawn moves and knight strategies between changing diapers for my two children.

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:700/0*mK0UJ3JvZO6L8XoX)

Photo by [Daniel Álvasd](https://unsplash.com/@danielalvasd?utm_source=medium&utm_medium=referral) on [Unsplash](https://unsplash.com/?utm_source=medium&utm_medium=referral)

Chess feels nostalgic. It reminds me of begging my mom to play games I always lost.

I don't play to become a grandmaster. I play for the challenge. I enjoy predicting moves, failing, and finding the occasional solution. "The Queen's Gambit" made the game look stylish, but the online community keeps me hooked. I spend hours on Reddit reading about the Sicilian Defense or laughing at memes about bishops.

The game offers a rare zen moment. I sit with the board and a ticking clock. This focus provides relief from constant phone notifications, even when players half my age checkmate me.

Life in my late 20s mirrors the board. I feel like I am coasting until a curveball arrives. Just as I think I have figured out a plan, life shouts, "Checkmate!"

Starting chess is messy. You will lose often. But the game proves it is never too late to learn something new and find joy in a challenge.
